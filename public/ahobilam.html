Sacred History

The Legend of
Aho Bilam & Aho Balam

Ahobilam marks the exact site of the holy legend where Lord Narasimha Swamy emerged from the stone pillar of Hiranyakashipu's palace to protect his five-year-old child devotee, Prahlada. The name itself reflects two divine definitions:

“Aho Bilam” (Oh, what a great cave!) — uttered by the devas in awe of the mysterious cave crevices in the hills where Lord Narasimha resided.
“Aho Balam” (Oh, what majestic strength!) — chanted by the devotees in praise of the infinite power of the half-man, half-lion incarnation.

Divided into **Lower Ahobilam** and **Upper Ahobilam**, the shrine complex nestles inside rugged limestone cavern walls, pristine cascading mountain rivers like Bhavanasini, and dense forests where ancient sages did deep penance.

Divine Pantheon

The Nine Shrines of Narasimha

Explore the nine distinct temples representing the nine unique divine aspects of the Lion God, scattered deep across the Nallamala forests.

🛕
Form 1

Ahobila Narasimha

The main self-manifested (Swayambhu) deity, represented in a fierce, majestic form deep inside a natural cave structure. Believed to be the original place of penance.

Trek: Base / Upper
🐗
Form 2

Kroda (Varaha) Narasimha

A unique manifestation depicting the combination of Lord Varaha (the Boar) and Lord Narasimha. Highly sacred for removing all planetary hurdles.

Trek: 1 km from Upper
🔥
Form 3

Jwala Narasimha

Located on a high cliff edge in Upper Ahobilam. The exact spot of ultimate rage where Hiranyakashipu was slain and the divine blood washed in Raktha Kunda.

Trek: Steep Cliff Trek
❤️
Form 4

Malola Narasimha

A peaceful (Soumya) form where the Lord is seen in a loving aspect with Goddess Lakshmi sitting on His lap. "Maa" means Lakshmi, "Lola" means beloved.

Trek: 1.5 km (Paved path)
🧘
Form 5

Yogananda Narasimha

Depicts the Lord in an auspicious yogic posture (dhyana mudra). Legendary tales link this shrine as the place where Prahlada received his spiritual education.

Trek: 2 km from Lower
🌳
Form 6

Chatravata Narasimha

Deity seated beneath a sacred "Chatravata" (peepal) tree canopy. The Lord is represented with a beautiful smile, pleased by divine musicians Hahala and Huhu.

Trek: 3 km from Lower
🐆
Form 7

Pavana Narasimha

Set in a remote jungle valley. Devotees traverse severe deep-forested terrain to offer their respects. Jeep transportation is highly recommended for safety.

Trek: 6 km forest trail
🏹
Form 8

Bhargava Narasimha

Named after Maharishi Bhargava (Parashurama), who performed intense penance at this deep-pond temple base to seek the glorious vision of Narasimha Swamy.

Trek: 2 km off-road
🕊️
Form 9

Karanja Narasimha

The deity is seated beneath the shade of a Karanja tree. Uniquely represented holding a bow, reflecting the divine vision granted to Lord Hanuman.

Trek: Road-facing access

Wilderness & Forest Safety Lock

Upper Ahobilam and remote forest trails close early at 03:00 PM - 05:00 PM. High-altitude trekking is prohibited after dark due to local Nallamala forest wildlife protection regulations.

Sacred Wilderness

The Pilgrimage Zones

Ahobilam is split into two distinctive altitude zones, requiring physical grit and devotion to conquer.

Base Camp Zone Easy to Moderate

Lower Ahobilam

Located at the base foothills. The legendary Lakshimidevi temple (Prahlada Varada Temple) stands here. Features smooth motorable roads, basic boarding amenities, ashrams, and serves as the launching base camp for all Upper Ahobilam trekking.

🦁

Bhargava, Yogananda, Chatravata Shrines are located in this lower circle, accessible via short walks.

🛕

Vijayanagara Gopurams show magnificent medieval Dravidian temple layouts with detailed stone carvings.

High-Altitude Cavern Zone Moderate to Difficult

Upper Ahobilam

The rugged mountain terrains, situated 8 kilometers higher. Devotees hike through deep, moist limestone caves, rocky river banks, and dense vegetation to find the powerful ancient cave temples. Requires professional guides.

⛰️

Ahobila, Kroda, Jwala, Malola temples reside here inside natural rock cracks and steep high cliff-sides.

🌊

Bhavanasini River Crossing requires stepping across slippery mountain rocks under forest canopies.

Pilgrimage Logistics

How to Reach Ahobilam

Ahobilam complex resides in Kurnool district, Andhra Pradesh. Base routes are well-connected by road networks.

By Air

Cuddapah Airport (110 km) is the closest base. Bengaluru International Airport (350 km) and Hyderabad Airport (360 km) are the closest major international gateways.

By Rail

Nandyal Railway Station (66 km) and Cuddapah Station (110 km) connect closely. Regular trains run from Chennai, Bengaluru, Vijayawada, and Hyderabad.

By Road

Direct buses ply from Kurnool, Nandyal, Hyderabad, and Cuddapah. Allachoti/Allagadda (24 km) serves as the nearest primary road junction point.

Sacred Enquiries

Frequently Asked Questions

The trek to Upper Ahobilam cave temples is moderate but steep in certain regions, involving natural rock formations, walking along river banks, and ascending staircase pathways. Proper trekking shoes and local forest guides are highly recommended.

Covering all nine shrines in one day is extremely difficult physically, as several temples (like Pavana and Jwala) require dedicated hikes or off-road travel. It is highly recommended to plan a relaxed 2-day pilgrimage to cover all shrines without exhaustively rushing.

The Nallamala forest range is a designated tiger reserve, home to leopards, wild boars, and langurs. The trails are completely safe during designated daylight pilgrimage timings (07:00 AM to 03:00 PM) as forest rangers and massive devotee crowds keep animals far from paved paths.

Jwala Narasimha marks the exact planetary spot where Lord Narasimha burst forth in His peak fiery wrath to slay the demon King Hiranyakashipu. Near the shrine is Raktha Kunda, a small pond whose waters are said to have retained a reddish hue where the Lord washed His holy hands.

Traditional decent wear is highly recommended. Dhotis, Kurtas, or Pajamas for men, and Sarees or Salwar Kameez for women. High comfort walking garments are advised due to trekking elements.

There is no age restriction, but elderly pilgrims or those with severe respiratory or knee issues should limit their trek to Lower Ahobilam shrines and opt for Jeep services to Pavana Narasimha rather than attempting the steep Jwala cliff climb.
